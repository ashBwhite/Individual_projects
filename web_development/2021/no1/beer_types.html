Lagers

Lagers are made with strains that do their fermenting at cold temperatures. They sit at the bottom of the liquid while they work. Lagers are also called bottom-fermented beers.
Lagers tend to be crisp and dry, with a special ability to quench thirst.

Pale Lager

Refreshingly crisp, light-bodied and moderately hoppy. Pairs easily with a wide variety of foods such as pizza.

Pilsner

Light-coloured, crisp lager which was invented in 19th century Europe. Hoppy and pale gold in colour Pairs good with lightly fried dishes such as tempura, calamari and cutlets.

Amber & Red Lager

Brewed with malted barley that is been toasted until colour gets to brown. Taste light and crisp as like other lagers but also contain caramel, toffee taste and toast aromas. Paris good with tacos.

Dark Lager

Still light bodied and have rich aromas including cholocate, coffee, molasses and pumpernikel bread taste. Pairs good with smoked fish.


Ales

Ales are made with yeast strains that prefer to ferment in warmer environment. The yeast floats on the surface while fermentation, ales are also called top-fermented beers.

Cream Ale

Pale in colour, light in flavour but not too bitter. Refreshing and equally versatile when it comes to pairing with grilled chicken dish.

Pale Ale

English style are malty and earthy, American style are hoppy taste and citrus overtones. Belgian style are sweeter and less hoppy but can be spicy. Pairs good with hamburgers.

Amber & Red Ale

Typically tate like caramel, dried fruit or even a hint of coffee. Pair good with slow-cooked and roasted foods such as jerk chicken.

India Pale Ale(IPA)

Originated in England during 19th century were made extra strong and hoppy to survive the journey to India. Usually have very bitter taste. Pairs good with food contains fat, spiciness and fruit such as meat dishes.

Dark Ale

English style are sweet and malty and Belgian style are little drier and possibly fruity. In general, dark ale have caramel notes, spice and sweet fruit aromas. Pairs good with savoury dishes.

Brown Ale

Have a sweet and nutty tasting. Pair good with all sorts of rich and sweet foods such as ribs to desserts.
